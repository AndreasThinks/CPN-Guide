Welcome to CPN 101! This document will guide you through the essential steps to issuing a Community Protection Notice (CPN). This guide can be used either as a training package, or as a guide following a stop. Before we start, you might want [[a brief introductions to CPNs and why you'd want to use them.|CPN Intro]] So, you see someone comitting anti-social behaviour - for the purpose of this example, we will assume this behaviour is also an offence, such as begging or illegally parking a rickshaw, however, a CPN can also apply for simple ASB, such as busking. The test for issuing a CPN is lower than other types of ASB legislation - you <i>do not need harassment, alarm and distress</i>. However, you must be satifised on reasonable grounds that the conduct of the subject:<ul> <li>is having a detrimental effect on the quality of life of those in the locality</li> <li>is persistent or continuing in nature</li> <li>is unreasonable</li> You have stopped your subject, and the above test is satisfied. You have obtained their details (if no separate offence is being comitted, remember your powers under <a href="http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2002/30/section/50"> S50 of the Police Reform Act</a>). What do you do? [[Consider a CPN - ask a colleague to check previous interactions on the spreadsheet|Spreadsheet Check]] [[Arrest for a separate substantive offence|Arrest for Begging]] [[Advise or use another disposal option|Advise]] You contact a colleague to check the ASB spreadsheet. The spreadsheet can be found on the S drive While checking the spreadsheet, MAKE SURE YOU ARE CHECKING ALL AVAILABLE TILES - there should be several thousand entries, not only a few lines. Based on what you find on the spreadsheet, pick one of the below options: Subject has [[never been encountered by police|CPN Written Warning]] Subject has [[been issued with a CPN written warning|CPN]] Subject has [[been issued with a CPN|CPN Breach]] And if you're not quite sure, [[issue a further written warning|CPN Written Warning]]. It can only provide more evidence going forward. For the purpose of this example, we will assume the offence is of begging. Begging is an offence contrary to the Vagrancy Act 1824. If you have a reason for arrest, and you believe it is proportionate, legal, accountable and necessary, go ahead. However, be aware that it is a relatively minor offence, and likely to result only in fines (which your suspect may not be able to pay). Consider whether this is a reasonable use of police time - are there aggravating factors to consider? Or do you simply want to issue a CPN on this occasion? Either way, you should always check for previous ASB interactions - you can do this either on the street as an alternative to arrest, or in custody following arrest. <b>In summary, even if you arrest, make sure you check the ASB spreadsheet and consider a CPN - we have "two bites of the apple" here, and we should use both of them.</b> To do this, you will [[check the ASB spreadsheet|Spreadsheet Check]]. Before anybody can be issued with a community protection notice, they must be issued with a written warning. What that warning needs to contain is flexible - "the written warning must make clear to the individual that if they do not stop the anti-social behaviour, they could be issued with a CPN." Beyond that however, what is written down is flexible: <a url="https://www.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/352562/ASB_Guidance_v8_July2014_final__2_.pdf">the guide for frontline professionals</a> states " it could be a standard form of words, adaptable to any situation – for instance, a pre-agreed form of words that can be used by the officer on the spot." In other words, if you just scribble something on a piece of paper explaining they need to stop or risk being issued a CPN, that's fine. However, pre-written warnings are available on the ORB S drive for rickshaw and begging, in both English and Romanian. Where possible, please use these templates. The written warning you are issuing is a piece of evidence - if you do not provide continuity, we cannot use it down the line. As such, make sure you do the below:<ul> <li>write a statement</li> <li>where possible, obtain a signed copy of the written warning. If not, take a picture of the warning you have issued</li> <li>Create a CRIMINT and attach the statement and copy of the warning</li> <li>Update the ASB spreadsheet with all actions taken</li> </ul> Doing the above is EXTREMELY IMPORTANT. If nobody knows you've issued the warning, we can't use it. That being said, keep in mind <b>a written warning is not a formal legal document</b>. If it isn't perfect, it's fine. As long as you have a statement logging that a warning was given, advising them to desist and explaining they risk a CPN, you'll be okay. Once the warning has been issued, you need to give the person reasonable time to rectify their behaviour. A warning is in place <i>forever</i> - so if you ever encounter this person again, you will go straight to [[issuing a CPN|CPN]]. Otherwise, you're all done! Congratulations on issuing a written warning. You can quit here, or [[start over|CPN 101]]. So, if your suspect has already been issued with a written warning at any point in the past, you can formally issue a Community Protection Notice. Congratulations! Like a written warning, what needs to be included in a CPN is relatively flexible. A CPN can be drafted from scratch if necessary so that it is appropriate to the situation and can include any or all of the following: • A requirement to stop doing specified things; • A requirement to do specified things; • A requirement to take reasonable steps to achieve specified results That being said, CPNs are a low level ASB tool - they should not be used to impose serious or long term conditions. Pre-written CPNs for begging and rickshaws are available on the ORB S: drive, and should be used whenever possible. These are available in English and Romanian, last three months, and have been designed in colaboration with CPS to make sure they are enforcable in court. Unlike written warnings, CPNs are evidential, legal documents - if your suspect goes on to breach the CPN, this interaction will be scrutinised. So, MAKE SURE YOU DOCUMENT EVERYTHING YOU DO! If you do not provide continuity, we cannot use it down the line. As such, make sure you do the below:<ul> <li>write a statement</li> <li>where possible, obtain a signed copy of the written warning. If not, take a picture of the warning you have issued</li> <li>Create a CRIMINT and attach the statement and copy of the warning</li> <li>Update the ASB spreadsheet with all actions taken</li> </ul> Doing the above is EXTREMELY IMPORTANT. If nobody knows you've issued the CPN, we can't use it. Congratulations! You've issued a CPN. It will remain valid for three months (or whatever period you specified). [[If your suspect continues the behaviour, they breach their CPN, which is an offence. Click here to explore that avenue.|CPN Breach]] If not, you're all done! You can quit, or [[click here to start over.|CPN 101]] If your suspect has breached the conditions of a previously issued CPN, they have comitted an offence <b>in addition to any substantive offences they may be comitting</b> - so, begging while a CPN is "in play" will result in both a charge for breach of CPN, and for begging. <i>Make sure your CPN is still valid. If it has expired in the meantime, you will have to re-issue a further CPN</i>. The easiest way to deal with both of those offences is to arrest, and deal with both in custody, although as with all arrest, IDCOPPLAN must apply. You will be arresting them for both begging and the breach of CPN. If you have no reasons for arrest, both offences can still be dealt with by postal charge. <b>The breach of the CPN gives you grounds for applying for a Criminal Behaviour Order (CBO).<br><u> It is essential that you link up with your sergeant, DWO, or ASB spoc to make sure this is applied for, as this is our best tool to deter antisocial behaviour</b></u> <b>So, you should be <ul> <li>charging the suspect for begging</li> <li>charging the suspect breach of CPN</li> <li>applying for a CBO</li> </ul></b> Applying for a CBO is relatively complicated, so again, seek advice. If you want more information, we have produced <a href="CBO Guide.doc"> a guide you can access from here</a>or you can read the <a href="ASB_Guidance_v8_July2014_final__2_.pdf">surprisingly good Home Office guidance</a>. It is incredibly important we get this right. We cannot arrest our way out of begging problem without using these tools correctly. Thanks for reading! Hopefully, you should now understand the procedure for CPNs. Before leaving, please , [[return to the start|CPN 101]], or [[read our FAQ|FAQ]]. So, why a CPN? Arrests for low level anti-social behaviour problems generally result in low-level punishments in the courts - eg, a fine that will never be repaid, or at worst a short custodial sentence. While these can have an impact of offender behaviour, it remains a short term solution. In addition, they are a huge drain on our time - the custody and court process are long and expensive. A CPN allows you to quickly and painlessly require the subject of the notice to adhere to conditions, that can be either positive - eg, you will attend courses - or negative - eg, stop loitering on this street - and if they fail to comply, will result in an offence, <i>in addition</i> to any other offence they may be comitting, such as begging. In addition, a breach of a CPN are good grounds to apply for a Criminal Behaviour Order (CBO), which allows us to impose very strict conditions (such as exclusions from certain post-codes), and will result in hefty custodial sentences if breached.
